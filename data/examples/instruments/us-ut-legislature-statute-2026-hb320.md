## Summary

HB 320 substantially restructured Chapter 72 (the Utah AI Policy Act) and introduced a second instrument type — the Joint Interpretation Agreement (JIA) — alongside the existing Regulatory Mitigation Agreement (RMA). Key changes:

- **Joint Interpretation Agreement (JIA) instrument.** OAIP may now enter agreements that interpret how existing law applies to AI without waiving the law (§13-72-401). Unlike an RMA, a JIA creates no waiver — it provides a written interpretive position that parties can rely upon while OAIP exercises regulatory discretion.
- **Expanded counterparties.** Agreements may now involve the judiciary, higher education institutions, and political subdivisions — not only executive-branch agencies (§13-72-401).
- **Extended term.** The maximum agreement term was extended from 12 months (with no renewal) to 12 months plus two 12-month extensions — up to 36 months total (§13-72-403).
- **Mandatory audits.** OAIP "shall perform regular audits" while an agreement is active (§13-72-401(6)).
- **Five-prong eligibility.** Learning Lab participants must demonstrate technical capability, financial resources, substantial consumer benefits outweighing risks, a risk-monitoring plan, and appropriately-limited scope (§13-72-402).

The restructure formalizes Utah's two-instrument architecture — RMA (waiver) and JIA (interpretation) — that PubLedge uses as the prototypical pattern for fact-specific written interpretations between a regulator and a regulated party.
