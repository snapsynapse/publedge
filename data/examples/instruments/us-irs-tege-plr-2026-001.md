## Summary

The IRS Tax Exempt and Government Entities division issued a final adverse determination that a redacted applicant organization does not qualify for exemption from federal income tax as a §501(c)(3) charitable organization. The organization failed both the organizational test (its Articles of Incorporation do not limit purposes to exempt activities and do not dedicate assets to exempt purposes upon dissolution) and the operational test (it devotes a substantial portion of its activities to commercial-type facility leasing for any interested party, and its fundraising activity benefits a for-profit entity controlled by one of its own Directors). No protest was filed within the 30-day window following the proposed adverse determination, making the determination final.

## Background

The organization (identity redacted per IRC §6110) applied for recognition of tax-exempt status under IRC §501(c)(3). It described itself as formed to empower neighborhoods by enhancing lives, building wealth, and providing support to those harmed by injustice, with a stated primary mission of addressing food insecurity. The organization controlled most shares of a former production facility (referred to as "the Facility" in the redacted letter, substituted as variable "C") that it operates as a shared coworking office space in an economically challenged neighborhood described as the poorest zip code in its city.

The Facility is owned by a for-profit LLC (variable "E"), 90% owned by an individual ("F") who also serves as the organization's Director. The organization planned to eventually acquire the Facility from E as part of a financial plan involving tax credits, loans, and fundraising. Two other individuals who own E also lease space in the Facility at market rates. The organization's stated future plans included renovating the Facility into a mixed-use destination: coworking space, cooperative resident-owned grocery store, business offices, public market, cultural and educational offerings, and a rooftop venue.

The IRS issued a proposed adverse determination (Letter 4034, dated November 12, 2025). The organization did not file a protest within the required 30 days. The proposed determination accordingly became final (Letter 4038, dated January 12, 2026) and was released publicly on April 3, 2026 under IRC §6110.

## Question presented

Whether an organization formed to empower an economically challenged neighborhood qualifies for exemption under IRC §501(c)(3) when its Articles of Incorporation do not limit its purposes to exempt activities, its assets are not dedicated to exempt purposes upon dissolution, it leases commercial facility space to any interested party without restriction, and its fundraising activity will benefit a for-profit entity owned by its own Director.

## Holding

No. The organization fails both the organizational test and the operational test under IRC §501(c)(3).

**Organizational test failure:** As a corporation, the formation document is the Articles of Incorporation, not the Bylaws. See Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(2). The Articles neither limit the organization's purposes to §501(c)(3) activities (required by Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(1)(i)) nor dedicate assets to an exempt purpose upon dissolution (required by Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(b)(4)). That the Bylaws contain the required provisions does not cure the deficiency in the Articles.

**Operational test failure:** A substantial portion of the organization's activities are devoted to non-exempt functions, disqualifying it under Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(1). Three distinct grounds support this conclusion, each independently disqualifying:

| Ground | Authority | Analysis |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial leasing to anyone | Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(1); *B.S.W. Group*, 70 T.C. 352 (1978); *Airlie Foundation*, 283 F. Supp. 2d 58 (D.D.C. 2003) | Providing coworking/office space to any interested party without charitable restriction is a substantial non-exempt activity; competition with commercial landlords is evidence of nonexempt predominance |
| Fundraising benefiting a for-profit entity | Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(1)(ii) | The organization's fundraising will benefit E while E still owns the Facility; E is controlled in part by the organization's own Director; this serves private interests, not a public benefit |
| Failure to demonstrate exclusive exempt purpose | *Better Business Bureau*, 326 U.S. 179 (1945); *New Dynamics Foundation*, 70 Fed. Cl. 782 (2006) | A single substantial non-exempt purpose bars exemption regardless of any exempt purposes present; gaps in the administrative record are resolved against the applicant |

The organization is unlike entities that qualified under Rev. Rul. 74-587 (below-cost loans with employment preference for residents) and Rev. Rul. 76-419 (industrial park requiring lessees to hire unemployed residents), because it imposed no charitable restrictions on its leasing activity and provided no documentation that businesses within its development would provide employment or other assistance to residents. It resembles Rev. Rul. 77-111, in which constructing a retail center with commercial tenants was held to benefit the businesses rather than exclusively accomplish §501(c)(3) purposes.

## Reliance scope

Per IRC §6110(k)(3), a written determination released under §6110 "may not be used or cited as precedent" by any taxpayer other than the one to whom it was issued. `reliance_scope: requesting-party-only` captures this statutory bar. Third parties may read the determination as a signal of IRS reasoning on the organizational/operational tests and private inurement doctrine, but cannot claim reliance.

## Limitations

- Binding only on the redacted applicant organization on the represented facts. Determines non-qualification, not criminality or any other penalty.
- Does not address whether a reorganized version of the entity (with corrected Articles and restricted leasing activity) could qualify.
- Does not address state or local tax treatment.
- The proposed determination (Letter 4034) was the substantive analytical letter; Letter 4038 is merely the finalization notice after the protest window closed. Both are enclosed in the released PDF but serve distinct functions.
- Case law cited (Better Business Bureau, B.S.W. Group, etc.) is incorporated by reference; this determination does not independently analyze those precedents.

## Notes on this demonstration remap

This PLR exercises four features not demonstrated by the three existing reference remaps (SEC NAL, CFPB AO, PLR-202506001):

1. **`obligation_kind: [restriction]` in a PLR** — PLR-202506001 was `[permission]` (a favorable ruling). This is the first adverse determination in the registry, showing that PLRs can carry `[restriction]` when the IRS denies a requested position. The prior note in PRIOR-ART.md that the three remaps cover "every value of `obligation_kind`" requires qualification: `[restriction]` was already present in the CFPB remap, but this adds an adverse PLR instance of that value, demonstrating the schema works symmetrically for denials.

2. **Dual-letter structure (proposed + final)** — IRS adverse exemption determinations are issued in two phases: a proposed adverse determination (Letter 4034) with a 30-day protest window, followed by a final adverse determination (Letter 4038) if no protest is filed. Both letters appear in the released §6110 PDF. The `enacted` date maps to the final letter; the proposed letter date is narrative context only.

3. **Private inurement / private benefit ground** — the operational test failure here rests in part on Treas. Reg. §1.501(c)(3)-1(d)(1)(ii): the organization's fundraising benefited a for-profit entity controlled by one of its own Directors. This private-benefit analysis is distinct from the profit-sharing analysis in PLR-202506001 and represents a new legal concept in the registry's coverage.

4. **Different issuing office** — PLR-202506001 was issued by IRS Office of Chief Counsel. This determination was issued by IRS Tax Exempt and Government Entities / Exempt Organizations Rulings and Agreements (signed by the Director). The `authority: irs-tege` slug is new; this adds a second distinct IRS authority to the registry and demonstrates that the `authority:` field distinguishes sub-agency offices, not just agencies.

Fields left null that a future lifecycle event could fill: `superseded_by` (if the organization reapplied and obtained a favorable ruling under a corrected organizing document), `withdrawn_date` (not applicable to adverse determinations — the determination stands unless reversed on appeal or in court under IRC §7428).
