## Summary

During the Dentacor demonstration period, the Utah Division of Professional Licensing (DOPL) forgoes enforcement of unlawful and unprofessional-conduct actions under Utah Code §58-69-5 solely for conduct authorized by the RMA. Enforcement remains in full force for any conduct outside the mitigation scope.

## What Counts

- Conduct that would otherwise be unlawful or unprofessional under §58-69-5
- Conduct authorized by the Dentacor RMA (AI-concurrent hygienist diagnosis + authorized procedures)
- Conduct occurring within the agreement period, within agreed geography and user scope
- Participant in full compliance with all RMA terms at the time of the conduct

## What Does Not Count

- Conduct outside the mitigation scope (other dental procedures, other patients, other periods)
- Conduct after termination of the agreement
- Conduct that violates other statutes or agreement terms (full penalty framework re-applies)

## Statute Anchors

- Utah Code §58-69-5 — Unlawful and unprofessional conduct (forbearance target)
- Utah RMA 2025-001 Section 15(D) — Enforcement forbearance
