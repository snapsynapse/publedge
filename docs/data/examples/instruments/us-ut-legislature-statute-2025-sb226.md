## Summary

SB 226 relocated and narrowed Utah's generative AI disclosure framework. The original §13-2-12 disclosure requirement (enacted by SB 149 in 2024) was repealed and re-enacted as a new Chapter 75 with substantive changes:

- **Narrower trigger.** The 2024 on-request standard was rewritten to require a consumer's "clear and unambiguous" request for AI disclosure (§13-75-103(1)).
- **New safe harbor.** Providing a clear and conspicuous disclosure at the outset of consumer interactions, and throughout, eliminates enforcement exposure under §13-75-104.
- **High-risk-occupation proactive tier.** For "high-risk AI interactions" (§13-75-101(5)) — sensitive data collection or personalized advice in finance, legal, medicine, or mental health — disclosure must be proactive: verbal at the start of oral exchanges, written before written exchanges (§13-75-103(2)-(3)).
- **Civil AI-assisted-violation liability.** §13-75-102 codifies the civil mirror of §76-2-107: "not a defense" that GenAI made the violative statement or was used in furtherance of the violation.

### Talking Point

> "SB 226 (2025) narrowed the original Utah on-request disclosure to require a 'clear and unambiguous' consumer prompt, added an outset+throughout safe harbor, and re-sited the civil mirror of the 'AI did it' defense at §13-75-102."
